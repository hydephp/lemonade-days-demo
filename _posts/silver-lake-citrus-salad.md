There is something about a citrus salad that feels like pressing pause on a busy afternoon. Maybe it's the way the sunlight catches every jewel-toned slice, or how the first crisp bite instantly cools you down after a morning spent wandering the farmers' market. This is the lunch I always come back to after strolling through Silver Lake, when the tote bag is full of fresh produce and the air still smells faintly of eucalyptus.

It's vibrant, refreshing, and surprisingly satisfying. The sweet-tart citrus, cool mint, and delicate crunch of fennel turn a handful of simple ingredients into something that feels effortlessly special. Pair it with a piece of crusty bread or enjoy it exactly as it is while the afternoon light pours through the kitchen window.

## The Secret Is in the Slicing

A little extra care with your knife makes all the difference here.

For the grapefruit and blood oranges, slice off both ends first so they sit flat on the cutting board. Trim away the peel and white pith by following the curve of the fruit, then slice into beautiful rounds or cut between the membranes for neat citrus segments.

The fennel should be sliced as thinly as possible. A mandoline works beautifully if you have one, but a sharp knife and a steady hand will give you wonderfully delicate ribbons that stay crisp without overpowering the salad.

## What You'll Need

- 1 ruby grapefruit
- 2 blood oranges
- 1 small fennel bulb
- A generous handful of fresh mint leaves
- Flaky sea salt
- Freshly cracked black pepper

### For the Dressing

- 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
- 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
- 1 teaspoon honey
- 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
- A pinch of flaky sea salt
- Freshly cracked black pepper

Whisk everything together until smooth and glossy.

## Bringing It All Together

Arrange the grapefruit and blood orange slices on a serving platter, letting the colors overlap naturally. Scatter the shaved fennel across the top, followed by plenty of torn mint leaves.

Drizzle the dressing over everything just before serving. Finish with flaky sea salt and a few twists of black pepper.

That's it.

Every bite is bright, crisp, and bursting with sunshine.

This is the kind of lunch that belongs on a shaded patio with an icy sparkling water nearby. The kind you pack for a picnic overlooking the reservoir, or make after a slow drive down the PCH with the windows down and your favorite playlist filling the car.

Simple ingredients. Golden light. A little taste of California.
