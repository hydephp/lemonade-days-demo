There’s something magical about those quiet early mornings before the city fully wakes up. The air is still cool, the light is soft, and the first sip of coffee feels like the beginning of a good day. This smoothie was inspired by mornings with the windows open, watching golden light spill across the kitchen counter while planning an easy drive down the PCH with nowhere particular to be.

Instead of pouring yesterday's cold brew over ice, freeze it into cubes. It's the little trick that keeps every sip rich and bold without watering anything down. Blended with a ripe banana and creamy oat milk, the result is chilled, naturally sweet, and just energizing enough to ease you into the day.

### You'll Need

- 1 cup cold brew coffee, frozen into ice cubes
- 1 ripe banana
- 1 cup oat milk
- Optional: a drizzle of maple syrup or a pinch of cinnamon

### How to Make It

Start by freezing your favorite cold brew in an ice cube tray overnight. Once frozen, add the coffee cubes to a blender with the banana and oat milk.

Blend until completely smooth and creamy. If you'd like it a little sweeter, add a drizzle of maple syrup. For a cozy twist, finish with a pinch of cinnamon before giving it one last quick blend.

Pour into your favorite chilled glass and enjoy right away while it's thick, frosty, and perfectly refreshing.

### A Bright Start

This is the kind of breakfast that doesn't ask much of you. It's quick enough for busy weekdays but feels special enough for slow weekends when the morning stretches out a little longer. Creamy, vibrant, and packed with coffee flavor, it's the easiest way to bring a little sunshine into your routine—no matter what's waiting on your calendar.
