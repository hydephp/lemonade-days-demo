There is a certain kind of summer afternoon when the air feels almost still. The errands are done, the windows are open, and the sunlight pours across the kitchen counter like honey. That's exactly when I start brewing this lavender haze iced tea.

It reminds me of driving down the PCH with the windows down, warm breeze carrying hints of wildflowers and saltwater, before ending the day watching golden hour settle over Silver Lake. It's light, floral, and just refreshing enough to make everything slow down for a minute.

The secret is keeping the lavender subtle. You want it to gently perfume the tea, not taste like a candle. Culinary lavender is wonderfully easy to find at farmers' markets, specialty grocery stores, or online. If you're lucky enough to forage your own, make absolutely sure it's culinary-grade and hasn't been treated with pesticides.

### What You'll Need

- 4 cups water
- 4 black tea bags
- 1 tablespoon culinary lavender buds
- 3 tablespoons honey or simple syrup (adjust to taste)
- 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
- Plenty of ice
- Lemon slices and fresh lavender sprigs for serving

### Brew It Right

Bring the water just to a boil, then remove it from the heat. Add the tea bags and lavender buds together and let everything steep for about five minutes. The tea should be rich and fragrant, while the lavender stays soft and delicate.

Remove the tea bags and strain out the lavender. Stir in the honey while the tea is still warm so it melts effortlessly, then add the fresh lemon juice for a little brightness.

Let the tea cool to room temperature before transferring it to the refrigerator. A couple of hours later, it will be perfectly chilled and ready for the best part.

### Serve Like Summer

Fill a tall glass with plenty of ice and pour the chilled tea over the top. Add a thin slice of lemon and, if you have one, a tiny sprig of lavender for that dreamy finishing touch.

The ice softens the floral notes just enough, leaving behind something crisp, refreshing, and lightly hazy. It's the kind of drink you make for friends lingering on the patio or simply for yourself after a long afternoon when the eucalyptus starts catching the breeze.

Sometimes the smallest rituals become the ones you look forward to most. A pitcher of homemade iced tea in the fridge has a way of turning an ordinary afternoon into something worth slowing down for.
