There are some recipes that instantly transport you somewhere else. This is one of them.

The first sip always reminds me of driving the PCH with the windows down, salt in the air, eucalyptus drifting through the canyons, and the kind of golden afternoon that makes you want to take the long way home. It's simple, bright, and completely unhurried.

The secret isn't fancy equipment or impossible-to-find ingredients. It's taking a few extra minutes to make a silky homemade simple syrup and letting fragrant Meyer lemons do what they do best. The result is crisp, vibrant, and perfectly balanced—never too sweet, never too tart.

This is the lemonade I make for slow weekends, backyard lunches, beach picnics, and those hazy evenings when everyone somehow ends up staying a little longer than planned.

## The Secret to Perfect Simple Syrup

If you've ever stirred sugar straight into cold lemonade only to find gritty crystals settling at the bottom, simple syrup is about to become your favorite kitchen trick.

By dissolving equal parts sugar and water over gentle heat, every sip stays perfectly smooth. It also lets the delicate floral notes of the Meyer lemons shine instead of competing with undissolved sugar.

The syrup keeps beautifully in the refrigerator for up to two weeks, so you can have fresh lemonade only minutes away whenever the weather calls for it.

## Ingredients

For the simple syrup:

- 1 cup granulated sugar
- 1 cup water

For the lemonade:

- 1 cup fresh Meyer lemon juice (about 6–8 Meyer lemons)
- 4 cups chilled water
- All of the cooled simple syrup
- Ice
- Thin lemon slices for serving
- Fresh mint (optional)

## Instructions

### 1. Make the simple syrup

Combine the sugar and water in a small saucepan over medium heat. Stir gently until the sugar has completely dissolved. There's no need to let it boil.

Remove from the heat and allow it to cool completely.

### 2. Juice the lemons

Roll the Meyer lemons on the countertop before slicing them in half. It's a tiny trick that helps release every last drop of their sweet, fragrant juice.

Strain out the seeds while keeping all that beautiful fresh flavor.

### 3. Mix everything together

In a large pitcher, combine the cooled simple syrup, fresh lemon juice, and chilled water.

Give everything a good stir, then taste. Prefer it brighter? Add another squeeze of lemon. Like it a little softer? Splash in a bit more cold water.

Lemonade should always taste like your perfect summer afternoon.

### 4. Serve ice cold

Fill glasses generously with ice and pour over the lemonade.

Finish with thin lemon slices, a sprig of fresh mint if you're feeling fancy, and enjoy immediately while everything is perfectly chilled.

## A Few Sunny Tips

- Meyer lemons create a softer, naturally sweeter lemonade with delicate floral notes. If you're using regular lemons, simply add an extra tablespoon or two of simple syrup to balance the tartness.
- Chill every ingredient before mixing for the coldest, most refreshing pitcher.
- Make the simple syrup the night before so everything comes together in just a few minutes.
- This recipe doubles beautifully for garden parties, family barbecues, or afternoons that slowly turn into dinner on the patio.

There is something wonderfully nostalgic about homemade lemonade. Maybe it's the sound of ice clinking in the glass, maybe it's the bright citrus aroma filling the kitchen, or maybe it's simply the reminder that the best summer moments are usually the simplest ones.
