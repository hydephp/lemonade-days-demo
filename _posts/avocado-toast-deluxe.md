There are some breakfasts that feel like a vacation, even if you're only stealing twenty quiet minutes before the day begins. This avocado toast is one of them.

It reminds me of those slow LA mornings when the streets are still sleepy, the air carries a hint of eucalyptus, and the sunlight pours through the kitchen windows just right. Maybe you're headed toward the beach, maybe you're planning a drive down the PCH with the windows down, or maybe you're simply staying home with your favorite playlist. Either way, this is the kind of breakfast that makes the whole day feel a little brighter.

### Why sourdough is everything

Every great avocado toast starts with the bread.

A thick slice of fresh sourdough gives you that perfect contrast: deeply golden and crisp on the outside while staying soft and airy in the middle. The slight tang balances the rich, buttery avocado, and every bite has just enough crunch to keep things interesting.

Toast it until the edges are deeply caramelized and the crust practically sings when you cut into it. It's worth waiting the extra minute.

### The dream team of toppings

The beauty of avocado toast is that simple ingredients really get their moment.

You'll need:

- Thick-cut sourdough bread
- One perfectly ripe avocado
- A squeeze of fresh lemon
- Flaky sea salt
- Freshly cracked black pepper
- A generous handful of microgreens
- A pinch of chili flakes
- Extra virgin olive oil

Mash the avocado gently with the lemon juice, salt, and pepper. Don't overwork it—you want plenty of texture. Spread it generously over the warm toast while it's still crisp.

Finish with a drizzle of olive oil, scatter the microgreens over the top, and add just enough chili flakes to wake everything up without overpowering the fresh flavors.

### Plating makes it feel special

Half the charm is in the presentation.

Serve your toast on a simple ceramic plate with a lemon wedge tucked off to the side. Add an iced coffee, a chilled lemonade, or a fresh fruit salad if you're feeling extra. The vibrant greens against the golden toast practically glow in the morning light, especially during golden hour in Silver Lake.

Sometimes I'll finish with an extra crack of black pepper and one last drizzle of olive oil just before serving. It catches the light beautifully and adds that little café touch.

Simple ingredients. Sunny mornings. A breakfast that feels both effortless and a little luxurious.

That's the kind of everyday magic we'll always make time for.
