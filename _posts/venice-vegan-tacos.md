There is something about wandering the Venice Beach boardwalk as the afternoon slips into golden hour. The air carries a hint of salt from the ocean, skateboard wheels hum across the pavement, and dinner somehow feels less like a plan and more like the next stop on a perfect summer evening. These tacos capture that feeling—a little bright, a little messy, and completely worth it.

They're the kind of dinner I crave after a long beach walk or a drive down the PCH with the windows down. Light enough to keep things easy, filling enough to satisfy, and packed with colors that somehow taste even better in the warm evening light.

## What You'll Need

For the roasted cauliflower:

- 1 medium head cauliflower, cut into bite-sized florets
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
- 1 teaspoon ground cumin
- ½ teaspoon garlic powder
- Salt and freshly cracked black pepper

For the mango salsa:

- 1 ripe mango, diced
- ½ cup finely diced red onion
- 1 jalapeño, finely chopped
- Juice of 1 lime
- A handful of chopped cilantro
- Pinch of salt

To serve:

- Small corn tortillas
- Shredded cabbage
- Sliced avocado
- Fresh lime wedges
- Extra cilantro

## Roast the Cauliflower

Preheat your oven to **425°F (220°C)**.

Toss the cauliflower with olive oil, smoked paprika, cumin, garlic powder, salt, and pepper until every floret is lightly coated. Spread everything onto a baking sheet in a single layer and roast for about **25–30 minutes**, turning halfway through, until the edges are deeply golden and just a little crisp.

Your kitchen should smell warm, smoky, and completely inviting by this point.

## Make the Mango Salsa

While the cauliflower roasts, stir together the diced mango, red onion, jalapeño, lime juice, cilantro, and a pinch of salt.

Let it rest for a few minutes so all those bright, zesty flavors mingle together. The sweet mango and fresh lime bring the perfect contrast to the roasted cauliflower.

## Assemble Your Tacos

Warm the tortillas in a dry skillet for about 30 seconds per side until they're soft and lightly toasted.

Layer each tortilla with a handful of shredded cabbage, plenty of roasted cauliflower, a generous spoonful of mango salsa, a few slices of creamy avocado, and another squeeze of fresh lime. Finish with extra cilantro if you're feeling fancy.

Dinner is served.

These tacos are best enjoyed outside if you can—on the patio, at the park, or anywhere the evening sun lingers a little longer. Pair them with sparkling water, your favorite citrus drink, or simply the soundtrack of distant waves and good conversation.
